Case Study 01

Therapy Pet Pals of Texas

Website re-building and re-branding of through UX/UI methodologies

As a dog lover with an incredibly sweet ESA, I first came across Therapy Pet Pals to volunteer my dog. As I realized that my 19 hour semester (at the time) wouldn't necessarily allow me to volunteer as much as I wanted to, I decided to find another way to help!

About the Organization

Therapy Pet Pals of Texas is a non-profit, 501(C)(3) volunteer organization based in Austin, TX that provides pet therapy for institutionalized individuals in healthcare facilities who often lack companionship and encouragement.

The Problem

The website’s design has not been touched since the 90s, so there is an immense need for a website redesign / rebranding. The clunky architecture prevents users from easily learning more about the history of the organization, how to volunteer, or how to donate.

Opportunities for improvement

  • Wide optimal line lengths throughout the site resulting in blocks of text that impede readability
  • Too many navigational buttons, leading to user confusion
  • Lack of responsive mobile and tablet versions of the site
  • Lack of visual separators on pages with more than two sections, confusing users with multiple versions of information (example: training dates for Houston vs. Austin)
  • Harsh colors that clash throughout the site

Design goal

My goal was to make the website more inviting and accessible, particularly for older visitors. I achieved this by implementing a warm, minimalistic design and simplifying navigation by making important links more prominent and intuitive.

Initial Questions

Who is the target audience?

  • People who want to volunteer
  • Organizations who are looking for TPPT’s services
  • A wide spectrum of people aged 20-65 years old

What are the main things that the audience looks for when they get to the site?

  • Volunteer and contact information, as well as information on how to donate

What are the Organizations goals?

  • To recruit and train as many volunteers they can in order to spread the philanthropy of pet therapy
